# Healthcare Resume Research Findings (2025-2026)

Research compiled for Billy Cruz's PCA and Transporter resume applications at Houston Methodist and other major hospital systems.

---

## 1. IDEAL RESUME STRUCTURE FOR PCA / TRANSPORTER ROLES

### Recommended Section Order (Top to Bottom)

1. **Header / Contact Information** - Full name, phone number, email, city/state (no full address needed). Include LinkedIn URL if available.
2. **Professional Summary** (2-3 sentences) - For career changers, this replaces a traditional "Objective." Highlight transferable skills and commitment to healthcare.
3. **Certifications & Licenses** - Place ABOVE work experience for healthcare roles. BLS/CPR, CNA, any relevant certifications. Include issuing organization and dates.
4. **Core Skills** - 8-12 targeted keywords pulled directly from the job posting. Mix of hard skills (vital signs, patient mobility, infection control) and soft skills (communication, empathy, teamwork).
5. **Professional Experience** - Reverse chronological. 3-6 bullet points per role. Action verbs + quantified results.
6. **Education** - Highest degree first. GPA only if 3.5+ and recent graduate.
7. **Additional** (optional) - Volunteer work, professional memberships, languages, awards.

### Why This Order Matters

Healthcare recruiters and ATS systems scan certifications first - it's the non-negotiable qualifier. Placing certs above experience ensures they're seen in the 7.4 seconds hiring managers spend on average reviewing a resume.

**Sources:** ResumeBuilder.com, GHR Healthcare, The Interview Guys, Covelo Group

## 2. ATS FORMATTING REQUIREMENTS

### Houston Methodist Uses iCIMS (NOT Workday)

**Critical finding:** Houston Methodist's career portal runs on **iCIMS** (careers-houstonmethodist.icims.com), not Workday. Methodist Health System (Dallas) uses Workday. This distinction matters because each ATS has different parsing behavior.

### iCIMS-Specific Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| **File Format** | DOCX is safest. iCIMS parses Word docs more reliably than PDFs. Design-heavy PDFs often fail. |
| **Layout** | Single-column ONLY. No two-column layouts, no tables, no text boxes. |
| **Section Headers** | Use standard, predictable names: "Professional Experience" (not "Where I've Made an Impact"), "Education" (not "Academic Journey"), "Skills", "Certifications" |
| **Date Format** | Pick ONE format and use it consistently throughout (e.g., "Jan 2020 - Present" everywhere). Mixed formats break parsing. |
| **Certifications** | MUST be in a dedicated section, never buried inside Education or Experience. Include both full name AND acronym: "Basic Life Support (BLS)" |
| **Contact Info** | Do NOT put in document header/footer - iCIMS cannot read header/footer content. Put directly in body text. |
| **Font** | Standard fonts only: Calibri, Arial, or Times New Roman |
| **Font Size** | 10-12pt for body text, up to 14pt for section headers |
| **Margins** | 0.5" to 1" on all sides |
| **No graphics** | Zero images, icons, charts, logos, progress bars, or decorative elements |
| **Prefill check** | After uploading, ALWAYS verify the prefilled fields - iCIMS silently truncates long entries |

### General ATS Statistics

- Over **98% of large organizations** use ATS to filter resumes
- Approximately **75% of applications** get automatically rejected before a human sees them
- Upload resume file rather than copy-paste (prevents hidden formatting issues)

**Sources:** ProfileOps (iCIMS parsing rules), Princive (iCIMS guide), BluePipes (nursing ATS tips), The Interview Guys

## 3. PAGE LENGTH, FONT, AND LAYOUT

### Page Length: ONE PAGE

For Billy's situation (5+ years of work experience but transitioning into healthcare as entry-level), the consensus is clear: **one page**.

**Reasoning:**
- Career changers should use one page because only *healthcare-relevant* content matters
- Entry-level healthcare positions (PCA, Transporter) expect concise resumes
- The "half-page rule": only go to two pages if you can fill at least half of page two with *relevant, impactful* content
- Two pages is only for 10+ years of directly relevant experience
- Hospital recruiters for support roles process high volumes - brevity wins

### Font Specifications

| Element | Specification |
|---------|--------------|
| **Font family** | Calibri (preferred), Arial, or Times New Roman |
| **Body text** | 10-11pt |
| **Section headers** | 12-14pt, bold |
| **Name** | 14-16pt, bold |
| **Line spacing** | Single within sections, extra space between sections |
| **Margins** | 0.75" to 1" (0.5" minimum if tight on space) |

### Layout Rules

- Single column, top to bottom flow
- Clear visual hierarchy with bold section headers
- Consistent bullet points (round or square, not decorative)
- Adequate white space - don't cram
- No color (black text on white background is safest for ATS and printing)

**Sources:** Scale.jobs, ResumeBuilder.com, The Interview Guys, Covelo Group

## 4. CAREER CHANGER STRATEGY (Non-Clinical to Healthcare)

### The Core Approach: Reframe, Don't Hide

Previous experience should be **repositioned** to highlight transferable skills, not omitted. Healthcare employers value diverse backgrounds.

### Translation Framework

| Previous Role | Healthcare Translation |
|--------------|----------------------|
| Customer service | De-escalation, communicating with stressed individuals, patient-facing skills |
| Retail/food service | Fast-paced environment, multitasking, standing for long shifts, team coordination |
| Warehouse/physical labor | Physical stamina, safety compliance, equipment handling, time-sensitive tasks |
| Any supervisory role | Team coordination, training, accountability, conflict resolution |
| Administrative work | Confidentiality handling (maps to HIPAA), scheduling, documentation |

### Professional Summary for Career Changers

**Do this:**
> "Dedicated professional transitioning into patient care, bringing 5+ years of experience in fast-paced, team-oriented environments. Strong communicator with proven ability to assist diverse populations under pressure. BLS-certified with a genuine commitment to compassionate, high-quality patient support."

**Not this:**
> "Hardworking individual looking for a job in healthcare."

### What Healthcare Recruiters Want to See from Career Changers

1. **Why healthcare?** - The summary should signal genuine motivation, not desperation
2. **Transferable skills framed in healthcare language** - "Assisted 50+ customers daily" becomes "Provided direct support to 50+ individuals daily in high-volume environment"
3. **Any healthcare-adjacent training** - BLS/CPR, first aid, volunteer work, shadowing
4. **Soft skills with evidence** - Don't just list "empathy" - show it through specific examples
5. **Physical capability** - For PCA/Transporter roles, mention comfort with physical demands

**Sources:** Kaplan Community, GHR Healthcare, Covelo Group, MyPerfectResume

## 5. COMMON MISTAKES ON HEALTHCARE RESUMES

### The Top Mistakes (Ranked by Impact)

1. **Using generic language instead of specific clinical terminology**
   - BAD: "Helped patients"
   - GOOD: "Assisted 15-20 patients daily with mobility, hygiene, and vital sign monitoring"

2. **Not tailoring the resume to each job posting**
   - Each application should mirror the exact language from that specific job description
   - If the posting says "patient transport" don't write "moving patients"

3. **Listing duties instead of accomplishments**
   - BAD: "Responsible for customer service"
   - GOOD: "Resolved 95% of customer concerns on first contact, serving 40+ individuals per shift"

4. **Missing or buried certifications**
   - BLS/CPR must be immediately visible, not on page 2 or buried in a paragraph

5. **Fancy formatting that breaks ATS**
   - Two-column layouts, graphics, icons, tables, colored backgrounds, non-standard fonts

6. **Vague professional summary or no summary at all**
   - This is your 7.4-second pitch - make it count

7. **Including irrelevant personal information**
   - No age, marital status, photo, religion, or social media (unless LinkedIn)

8. **Spelling and grammar errors**
   - ATS parsers rely on correct spelling to match keywords. "Pateint care" won't match "patient care"

9. **Inconsistent date formatting**
   - Especially deadly for iCIMS - pick one format and stick with it

10. **Not including both acronym AND full name for certifications**
    - Write "Basic Life Support (BLS)" not just "BLS" - covers both human and ATS scanning

**Sources:** Teal HQ, ResumeBuilder.com, Resume Worded, VisualCV, ProfileOps

## 6. KEYWORDS TO TARGET

### For Patient Care Assistant (PCA) Roles

**Hard Skills:**
- Patient care / direct patient care
- Vital signs monitoring
- Activities of daily living (ADLs)
- Patient mobility / ambulation assistance
- Infection control / hand hygiene
- Medical terminology
- Electronic Health Records (EHR)
- HIPAA compliance
- Basic Life Support (BLS)
- CPR / First Aid
- Blood pressure / pulse / temperature
- Patient safety
- Fall prevention
- Specimen collection
- Intake and output (I&O)

**Soft Skills:**
- Compassionate care
- Team collaboration
- Communication (verbal and written)
- Time management
- Attention to detail
- Cultural sensitivity
- Adaptability
- Reliability / dependability

### For Hospital Transporter Roles

**Hard Skills:**
- Patient transport / patient transfer
- Wheelchair / stretcher / gurney operation
- Equipment sanitization
- Safety protocols
- Oxygen tank handling
- IV pole management
- Specimen / lab transport
- Medical equipment transport
- Infection control
- Body mechanics / safe lifting

**Soft Skills:**
- Navigation / wayfinding
- Time management / punctuality
- Patient interaction / bedside manner
- Physical stamina / endurance
- Urgency / prioritization
- Team coordination

## 7. FINAL RESUME CHECKLIST

Before submitting to Houston Methodist (iCIMS) or any hospital ATS:

- [ ] Saved as **.docx** (not PDF)
- [ ] **Single column** layout, no tables or text boxes
- [ ] **Standard fonts** (Calibri 10-11pt preferred)
- [ ] Contact info in **body text**, not in header/footer
- [ ] **Professional summary** tailored to this specific role (2-3 sentences)
- [ ] **Certifications** in dedicated section with full name + acronym
- [ ] **Dates are consistent** format throughout (e.g., "Month Year" everywhere)
- [ ] **Keywords from job posting** naturally woven into experience bullets
- [ ] Each bullet starts with **action verb** + includes **quantified result** where possible
- [ ] **One page** only
- [ ] No graphics, icons, images, or decorative elements
- [ ] No spelling or grammar errors
- [ ] After uploading to iCIMS, **verified all prefilled fields** are correct
